HAYAO MIYAZAKI

About Hayao Miyazaki

Hayao Miyazaki was born on January 5, 1941, in Tokyo, Japan. His father, Katsuji Miyazaki, was the director of Miyazaki Airplane, a company that produced parts for fighter planes during World War II. This early exposure to aviation influenced Miyazaki's lifelong fascination with flying, a theme that recurs in many of his films. Miyazaki's family moved frequently during his childhood due to the war. He developed a love for manga and animation at a young age, inspired by the works of Osamu Tezuka and Tetsuji Fukushima. Miyazaki attended Toyotama High School, where he began to seriously pursue his interest in animation. In 1963, he graduated from Gakushuin University with a degree in political science and economics, but his passion for animation led him to join Toei Animation as an entry-level animator. This marked the beginning of his illustrious career in the animation industry.

Studio Ghibli Animation Studio

As a co-founder of Studio Ghibli, Miyazaki has been instrumental in creating a studio that stands as a beacon of excellence in animation. Studio Ghibli's films are celebrated not just for their artistic beauty but also for their compelling stories and rich, multi-dimensional characters. Under his direction, the studio has produced some of the most beloved and critically acclaimed animated films in history, such as “My Neighbor Totoro”, “Spirited Away”, and “Princess Mononoke”. His works often explore themes of nature, humanity, and pacifism, blending fantasy with profound moral and philosophical questions. Miyazaki's influence on animation and film has earned him numerous awards and a dedicated global following.

Awards & Achievements

  • Academy Award for Best Animated Feature: For"Spirited Away" (2003).
  • Golden Lion Lifetime Achievement: At Venice Film Festival (2005).
  • Saturn Award: For Best Animated Film for "Howl's Moving Castle" (2006).
  • Academy Honorary Award: In 2014, for his impact on animation.
